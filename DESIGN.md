# CheckIn Voice — Design

A running record of intentional design choices for the voice layer, with rationale and source citations. Written deliberately rather than by intuition; updated as David works through Cathy Pearl's *Designing Voice User Interfaces* and as questions arise.

`PLAN.md` covers scope and sequencing. This document covers *what we are building, and why*.

Each entry below should answer: the question being decided, what the references say, what we decide, and why. Citations to chapter and section so future revisits stay grounded.

## References

**Tier A — directly relevant**

- Cathy Pearl, *Designing Voice User Interfaces* (O'Reilly, 2016) — primary text. David is reading.
- [Google — Conversation Design](https://developers.google.com/assistant/conversation-design/welcome) — closest free analogue to Pearl. Persona, cooperative principle, error recovery, prompt design, multimodal patterns. Pearl joined Google after writing the book; much of her thinking is reflected here.
- [Apple HIG — Siri](https://developer.apple.com/design/human-interface-guidelines/siri) — authoritative platform guidance for iOS. Less comprehensive than the textbooks, but every word matters because it shapes App Store review expectations.
- [WWDC 2020 — Evaluate and optimize voice interaction for your app](https://developer.apple.com/videos/play/wwdc2020/10071/) — 30 minutes, iOS-specific.

**Tier B — research-grounded supplements**

- [Nielsen Norman Group — voice topic](https://www.nngroup.com/topic/voice/) — classical usability principles applied to voice; especially good on voice signifiers and the discoverability problem.
- [Amazon Alexa Voice Design Guide](https://developer.amazon.com/en-US/alexa/alexa-haus/voice-fundamentals) — domain mismatch (far-field, command-based) but useful for prompt patterns and disambiguation.

**Tier C — author-direct**

- [Cathy Pearl's website](https://www.cathypearl.com/) — talks, articles, and current thinking since the 2016 book.
